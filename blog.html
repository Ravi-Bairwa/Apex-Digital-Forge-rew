What is SEO?

SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization. It is the process of improving a website so that it appears higher in search engine results pages (SERPs) — primarily Google — when people search for relevant keywords or phrases.

When someone types "best white label link building agency" into Google, Google's algorithm scans billions of web pages and ranks them in order of relevance and authority. SEO is the practice of making your website more relevant and authoritative so Google ranks it higher than your competitors.

03
On-Page SEO

On-page SEO refers to all the optimizations you make directly on your website pages to help search engines understand what your content is about and rank it appropriately. It is the foundation that everything else is built on.

📝 Content Optimization
  • • Target keyword in title tag and H1
  • • Keyword used naturally throughout content
  • • Related keywords and synonyms included
  • • Content depth matches search intent
  • • Minimum 800-1500 words for competitive terms
🏗️ Technical On-Page
  • • Meta title under 60 characters
  • • Meta description under 155 characters
  • • Clean URL structure (/services not /p?id=3)
  • • Internal links to related pages
  • • Image alt text with keywords
📊 Heading Structure
  • • One H1 per page only
  • • H2s for main sections
  • • H3s for subsections
  • • Keywords naturally in headings
  • • Logical hierarchy throughout
⚡ User Experience
  • • Page loads under 3 seconds
  • • Mobile responsive design
  • • Clear call to action
  • • Low bounce rate signals
  • • Schema markup for rich results

05
Technical SEO

Technical SEO ensures that search engines can find, crawl, understand, and index your website without obstacles. Even the best content and strongest backlink profile will underperform if Google cannot properly access and read your site.

✅ Core Technical Factors
  • • HTTPS (SSL certificate)
  • • XML Sitemap submitted to Google
  • • Robots.txt properly configured
  • • No broken links (404 errors)
  • • Canonical tags on all pages
  • • Structured data / Schema markup
⚡ Performance Factors
  • • Core Web Vitals (LCP, FID, CLS)
  • • Page speed under 3 seconds
  • • Mobile-first design
  • • Compressed images
  • • Clean URL structure
  • • No redirect chains

Why Backlinks Are Necessary

Backlinks remain the single strongest ranking signal in Google's algorithm after two decades of SEO. Google's original PageRank algorithm was built entirely on the idea that links from other websites represent votes of confidence. While the algorithm has evolved dramatically, the fundamental principle remains unchanged — more high-quality backlinks means higher rankings.

"Links are still the best way that we have to discover how pages are connected to each other and how we can determine which pages might be most important."
— Google Search Quality Senior Strategist

Domain Authority (DA) & Page Authority (PA) Explained

Domain Authority (DA) and Page Authority (PA) are metrics developed by Moz that predict how likely a website or page is to rank in search results. They are scored on a scale of 1 to 100 — higher is better. These are third-party metrics, not official Google scores, but they are widely used as proxies for site quality.

DA
Domain Authority

Measures the overall authority of an entire domain based on its backlink profile, age, and size. A site with DA 70+ has typically earned thousands of quality backlinks over many years. DA is used to evaluate the overall strength of a website as a link source.

PA
Page Authority

Measures the ranking strength of a specific individual page rather than the whole domain. A page with PA 60 on a DA 40 site can still pass significant link authority if it has earned strong backlinks directly to that specific URL.

⚠️ IMPORTANT: DA is NOT a Google metric

Google does not use DA or PA in its algorithm. These are Moz metrics used by SEOs as estimation tools. A site can have high DA but zero real traffic — which is why at Apex Digital Forge we always verify actual organic traffic alongside DA before placing any backlink.

Keyword Research

Keyword research is the process of identifying the exact words and phrases your target audience types into search engines. It is the strategic foundation of all SEO work — you cannot rank for terms you have not identified and targeted specifically.

🎯
Search Volume
How many people search this keyword per month. Higher volume = more potential traffic.
⚔️
Keyword Difficulty
How competitive it is to rank. New sites should target low difficulty keywords first.
💰
Search Intent
What the searcher actually wants. Informational vs commercial vs transactional.
FREE KEYWORD RESEARCH TOOLS

• Google Search Console — your actual rankings
• Ubersuggest — free keyword ideas

• Google Autocomplete — real search suggestions

Anchor Text Strategy

Anchor text is the clickable text of a hyperlink. When another website links to yours, the words they use as the link text send a signal to Google about what your page is about. Using the same keyword as anchor text on every backlink looks unnatural and can trigger algorithmic penalties.
